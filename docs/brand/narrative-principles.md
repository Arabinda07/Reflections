# Narrative Principles

This document defines what Reflections is, what it believes, what it refuses to become, and how all product decisions should be framed.

For surface-level execution rules, use `docs/brand/brand-in-practice.md`. This narrative document defines the belief system; Brand In Practice defines how that belief system appears on the website, in product UI, in AI copy, in privacy explanations, in Pro prompts, and in lifecycle surfaces.

## Core Narrative

Reflections is a private writing sanctuary for people who want to understand themselves more clearly.

The product begins with a simple act: a person writes. They may write a few lines or a long entry. They may name a mood, add tags, capture tasks, or simply put language around something that felt shapeless. The product's first job is to make that act feel safe, calm, and worth returning to.

Reflections can help the user notice patterns, revisit previous thoughts, and request AI-supported reflection. But those features must orbit the writing experience. They must never become the center of gravity.

## Product Promise

Reflections gives people a quiet place to write and, when they ask, a gentle way to understand the patterns inside that writing.

Use this promise to test every feature:

- If it makes writing easier, calmer, clearer, or more meaningful, it probably belongs.
- If it makes the user perform, compare, optimize, or feel monitored, it probably does not.
- If it uses AI to interrupt the user, impress the user, or claim authority over the user's life, it does not belong.

## The One-Sentence Position

Reflections is a calm wellness journal where writing comes first and optional AI helps you notice what your entries are beginning to reveal.

## The Long Position

Reflections is designed for private self-understanding. It combines long-form journaling, mood and tag patterns, gentle writing prompts, and optional AI reflections. The product avoids pressure mechanics like streaks, shame reminders, public sharing, productivity scoring, and attention-grabbing gamification. Its role is to protect a space where the user can think honestly.

## What Reflections Is

Reflections is:

- a private journal
- a writing-first wellness space
- a calm dashboard for patterns
- a companion for self-understanding
- a place to store and revisit inner life
- an optional AI reflection surface
- a product built around trust, consent, and emotional restraint
- a sanctuary for noticing, not a machine for optimizing

## What Reflections Is Not

Reflections is not:

- a therapist
- a diagnosis tool
- a crisis service
- a productivity tracker
- a habit game
- a social network
- an AI chatbot with a journal attached
- a motivational coach
- a streak app
- a mood surveillance system
- a place for generic inspirational content
- a content farm of prompts
- a product that treats private writing as data inventory

## The Main Character

The main character is the user writing.

Not the AI.
Not the analytics.
Not the subscription.
Not the interface.
Not the brand voice.

All surfaces should quietly support the user in staying with their own thoughts.

## The Emotional Contract

The user may arrive tired, scattered, hopeful, numb, reflective, anxious, or curious. The product should meet them without demanding a particular emotional state.

Reflections must never imply:

- "You should be doing better."
- "You failed to journal."
- "Your feelings are a problem to fix."
- "The app understands you better than you do."
- "AI has the final interpretation."
- "You need to upgrade to take your inner life seriously."

Reflections may imply:

- "You can begin exactly where you are."
- "Your writing belongs to you."
- "Patterns can emerge slowly."
- "You can ask for help when you want it."
- "Nothing needs to be forced."
- "The product can stay quiet until needed."

## Narrative Pillars

### 1. Writing First

The journal entry is the primary object. All features should protect the user's ability to write without distraction.

Actionable rules:

- Do not place AI suggestions inside the writing flow unless the user asks.
- Do not block saving because an AI feature failed.
- Do not make prompts feel mandatory.
- Do not turn the editor into a dashboard.
- Preserve a clear, focused writing area on mobile.

### 2. Optional Intelligence

AI is a tool the user reaches for. It should not behave like a background observer unless a specific, clearly explained product mode has been intentionally enabled.

Actionable rules:

- Use explicit verbs: "Reflect with AI", "Refresh with AI".
- Do not say "AI is watching", "AI learns you", or "AI monitors your mood".
- If AI is unavailable, the core writing product must still feel complete.
- AI output should be framed as a reflection, not a verdict.

### 3. Calm Control

The user should always understand what is happening and how to stop it.

Actionable rules:

- Give clear states for loading, failure, locked access, and empty content.
- Use plain language for privacy and AI.
- Avoid hidden automation.
- Make destructive actions explicit and reversible where possible.
- Keep settings understandable without technical fluency.

### 4. Private Meaning

Reflections treats personal writing as intimate material, not engagement fuel.

Actionable rules:

- No public sharing defaults.
- No social comparison.
- No leaderboards, streaks, XP, badges, or pressure loops.
- No pushy reminders that imply guilt.
- No copy that treats notes as "content".

### 5. Gentle Depth

The product can be emotionally intelligent without sounding dramatic, clinical, or mystical.

Actionable rules:

- Prefer grounded language over spiritual abstractions.
- Prefer observation over interpretation.
- Prefer "you may notice" over "this means".
- Do not over-name emotions the user did not name.
- Do not exaggerate insight from thin data.

### 6. Additive Premium

Pro should expand the sanctuary. It should not make free users feel punished.

Actionable rules:

- Free limits should be clear and calm.
- Upgrade prompts should be compact, factual, and low-pressure.
- Never imply that care, healing, or self-understanding requires payment.
- Pro language should focus on continuity, depth, and convenience.

## Product Narrative By Surface

### Landing

Purpose: explain Reflections quickly and calmly.

Should communicate:

- private writing space
- optional AI support
- gentle pattern awareness
- no pressure mechanics
- user ownership

Should avoid:

- hype
- "revolutionary AI"
- therapy claims
- productivity promises
- "unlock your best self"

Good direction:

- "A quiet place to write and notice what keeps returning."
- "Write privately. Reflect when you're ready."
- "Optional AI support stays out of the way until you ask."

Bad direction:

- "Transform your life with AI-powered journaling."
- "Never miss a day again."
- "Discover the hidden truth behind your emotions."

### Auth

Purpose: help the user enter without friction.

Should communicate:

- privacy
- simplicity
- continuity
- safety

Good direction:

- "Create your private writing space."
- "Sign in to return to your notes."

Bad direction:

- "Start optimizing your mental health."
- "Join thousands transforming their lives."

### Home

Purpose: help the user decide where to begin.

Should communicate:

- begin writing
- revisit recent notes
- view patterns
- keep the tone quiet

Good direction:

- "Begin with a few lines."
- "Your recent reflections are here when you want them."

Bad direction:

- "Crush today's journaling goal."
- "Your performance is up 20%."

### Create Note

Purpose: protect flow.

Should communicate:

- open space
- optional prompts
- clear save affordance
- AI only by explicit request

Good direction:

- Placeholder: "Start with what is here."
- Button: "Reflect with AI"
- Save state: "Saved."

Bad direction:

- Placeholder: "Tell AI how you feel today."
- Button: "Analyze me"
- Save state: "Your data has been processed."

### Insights

Purpose: help users notice rhythm, mood, tags, and patterns.

Should communicate:

- reflective overview
- signal grows over time
- no judgment
- low-entry states are normal

Good direction:

- "A little more writing will give this view something real to reflect."
- "Patterns will stay quiet until there is enough signal."

Bad direction:

- "Insufficient data."
- "You need to journal more."

### Life Wiki / Sanctuary

Purpose: organize compounding meaning from writing.

Should communicate:

- personal library
- grounded synthesis
- source-backed patterning
- user control

Good direction:

- "Your Life Wiki builds from the reflections you choose to include."
- "These pages should stay grounded in your own notes."

Bad direction:

- "Your AI knows who you are."
- "We automatically understand your life."

### Account / Pro

Purpose: explain access, control, and billing calmly.

Should communicate:

- current plan
- limits
- optional upgrade
- data controls

Good direction:

- "Pro keeps writing unlimited and lets you refresh AI-supported views whenever you ask."
- "The free plan includes enough room to begin."

Bad direction:

- "Upgrade now to continue your healing journey."
- "Your self-discovery is locked."

## Narrative Rules For AI

AI should behave like:

- a warm friend with a slight journal mirroring
- a careful reader
- a pattern-noticer
- a quiet assistant
- a second lens

AI should not behave like:

- a therapist
- a guru
- a judge
- a productivity coach
- a fortune teller
- a diagnostic engine
- a best friend that assumes intimacy

AI language must include uncertainty when appropriate:

- "This seems to point toward..."
- "One possible pattern is..."
- "You might be circling..."
- "If this feels true, it may be worth returning to..."

AI language must avoid false certainty:

- "You are..."
- "This proves..."
- "The reason is..."
- "Your trauma is..."
- "You need to..."

## Strategic Refusals

Reflections should refuse certain product directions even if they improve engagement metrics.

Do not build:

- streaks
- leaderboards
- public feeds
- "journal every day" guilt loops
- forced AI analysis
- automated emotional labeling without consent
- push notifications that shame absence
- dark-pattern paywalls
- clinical claims without clinical infrastructure
- engagement badges
- virality mechanics

Can build:

- gentle reminders if explicitly configured by the user
- calm writing prompts
- export and ownership tools
- low-pressure insights
- optional AI reflection
- pattern views with clear data limits
- accessibility improvements
- privacy and control surfaces
