# Voice

Voice is the stable personality of Reflections. Tone changes by situation, but voice stays consistent.

## Voice Summary

Reflections sounds calm, clear, intimate, grounded, and lightly reflective.

It should feel like a thoughtful human sitting beside the user, not like a brand performing warmth or an AI trying to be profound.

## The Five Voice Qualities

### 1. Calm

Calm means the product lowers pressure.

Calm sounds like:

- "Take your time."
- "Begin anywhere."
- "This can stay quiet for now."
- "You can come back to this later."

Calm does not sound like:

- "Don't lose your progress."
- "You are falling behind."
- "Complete today's reflection."
- "Keep your streak alive."

Rules:

- Use short sentences.
- Avoid urgency unless the situation is truly urgent.
- Avoid exclamation points in most UI.
- Avoid countdowns, pressure timers, and competitive language.
- Let empty states feel normal, not failed.

### 2. Human

Human means the product sounds written by a person who respects emotional nuance.

Human sounds like:

- "That saved. You can keep writing when you're ready."
- "There may not be enough here yet for a useful pattern."
- "I couldn't refresh this just now. Try again in a moment."

Human does not sound like:

- "Operation successful."
- "User input processed."
- "AI analysis unavailable."
- "Insufficient dataset."

Rules:

- Use contractions when they sound natural.
- Prefer plain verbs over technical verbs.
- Avoid corporate abstractions.
- Avoid generic wellness slogans.
- Write like a careful product person, not a marketing page.

### 3. Grounded

Grounded means the product does not overclaim.

Grounded sounds like:

- "A few themes may be emerging."
- "This reflection is based on the notes you've written here."
- "Patterns become more useful as you add more entries."

Grounded does not sound like:

- "We understand your inner world."
- "Unlock the truth about yourself."
- "Your AI companion knows you deeply."
- "Transform your mental health."

Rules:

- Say what the system actually knows.
- Use "may", "might", and "seems" when interpreting.
- Do not imply medical, therapeutic, or diagnostic authority.
- Do not make universal promises.

### 4. Spacious

Spacious means the product leaves room for the user's own meaning.

Spacious sounds like:

- "A thought to return to."
- "Something worth noticing."
- "This can be enough for today."

Spacious does not sound like:

- "Here is what this means."
- "Your next step is obvious."
- "You should address this immediately."

Rules:

- Do not over-explain inside UI.
- Do not fill every screen with guidance.
- Keep helper text concise.
- Prefer optional affordances over instruction-heavy surfaces.
- Let silence be part of the design.

### 5. Trustworthy

Trustworthy means the product is specific about privacy, AI, billing, and limits.

Trustworthy sounds like:

- "AI only runs when you choose this action."
- "Your notes are private to your account."
- "The free plan includes 30 notes each month."
- "You can still read what is already here."

Trustworthy does not sound like:

- "Bank-grade privacy" unless proven and defined.
- "Completely secure" unless technically guaranteed.
- "Free forever" unless legally true.
- "Unlimited" when quotas or provider limits exist.

Rules:

- Be concrete.
- Avoid vague reassurance.
- Do not hide limits.
- Do not imply background AI processing if the product is on-demand.
- Do not use fear to sell Pro.

## Voice Spectrum

Use these sliders to calibrate copy:

| Axis | Reflections Should Be | Reflections Should Not Be |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Energy | quiet | sleepy or lifeless |
| Warmth | kind | sentimental |
| Intelligence | thoughtful | superior |
| AI presence | optional | invisible-but-active |
| Wellness | supportive | clinical |
| Premium | refined | elitist |
| Simplicity | clear | thin or generic |

## Voice In Practice

### Buttons

Buttons should use direct, concrete verbs.

Good:

- "Write a note"
- "Save note"
- "Reflect with AI"
- "Refresh with AI"
- "See Pro options"
- "Delete account"
- "Export notes"

Avoid:

- "Begin journey"
- "Unlock clarity"
- "Analyze my soul"
- "Optimize mood"
- "Continue transformation"

### Placeholders

Placeholders should invite, not instruct.

Good:

- "Start with what is here."
- "A few lines are enough."
- "What do you want to remember about today?"

Avoid:

- "Tell us everything."
- "Describe your mental state."
- "Enter journal content here."

### Empty States

Empty states should normalize absence.

Good:

- "Nothing is here yet. Your notes will appear as you write."
- "This view needs a few entries before it can say anything useful."
- "Your Life Wiki will stay quiet until there is enough signal."

Avoid:

- "No data found."
- "You haven't created anything."
- "Start journaling to unlock insights!"

### Success States

Success should be quiet.

Good:

- "Saved."
- "Your note is safe."
- "Reflection refreshed."
- "Export ready."

Avoid:

- "Amazing work!"
- "You crushed it!"
- "Another step toward your best self!"

### Error States

Errors should be clear and non-blaming.

Good:

- "I couldn't save this just now. Please try again."
- "The Life Wiki couldn't refresh. Your notes are unchanged."
- "Your session expired. Sign in again to continue."

Avoid:

- "Invalid user action."
- "You failed to complete the form."
- "Something went wrong!!!"

### Loading States

Loading should set expectations.

Good:

- "Saving..."
- "Reflecting..."
- "Refreshing your Life Wiki..."
- "Gathering enough signal..."

Avoid:

- "Processing emotional intelligence model..."
- "Mining your data..."
- "Building your psychological profile..."

## AI Voice

AI reflection should sound like a careful reader of the user's writing.

Good:

- "One thing your entry seems to return to is the wish for more room."
- "There is a quiet contrast here: you sound tired, but also clear about what matters."
- "If you come back to this later, you might ask what felt heavy before the day even began."

Avoid:

- "You are experiencing avoidance behavior."
- "This indicates unresolved trauma."
- "You need to set boundaries immediately."
- "As an AI language model..."

## Legal And Privacy Voice

Legal/privacy copy should be plain and calm.

Good:

- "Your notes belong to you."
- "We do not use your notes to train AI models."
- "AI features run only when you choose them."
- "Some features require an internet connection."

Avoid:

- "We value your privacy" without details.
- "Your data is completely safe."
- "Our proprietary AI protects you."

## Monetization Voice

Pro should feel like continuity, not pressure.

Good:

- "Pro keeps writing unlimited and lets you refresh AI-supported views whenever you ask."
- "You can still read what is already here."
- "Upgrade when you want more room."

Avoid:

- "Upgrade to continue healing."
- "Your journey is locked."
- "Don't miss out on deeper self-awareness."
