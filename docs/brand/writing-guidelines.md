# Writing Guidelines

This document turns the Reflections voice into practical rules for product copy, marketing copy, AI prompts, legal summaries, and UI states.

## Core Writing Rules

1. Write for the user's nervous system, not for conversion.
2. Prefer clarity over cleverness.
3. Prefer invitation over instruction.
4. Prefer observation over judgment.
5. Prefer specific limits over vague promises.
6. Use AI language only when the user-facing behavior is actually AI-driven.
7. Never imply clinical, therapeutic, or diagnostic authority.
8. Never make absence feel like failure.

## Sentence Style

Use:

- short sentences
- simple verbs
- concrete nouns
- active voice
- contractions where natural
- enough warmth to feel human

Avoid:

- corporate abstractions
- clinical labels
- motivational slogans
- productivity language
- vague intensifiers
- mystical language
- engagement jargon

Good:

> Your Life Wiki needs 2 more entries before it can say anything useful.

Bad:

> Continue adding content to activate your personalized self-awareness experience.

## Feature Naming Rules

Names should be calm, literal, and easy to understand.

Good names:

- Reflections
- Notes
- My Notes
- Insights
- Life Wiki
- Reflect with AI
- Refresh with AI
- Monthly Wellness Journey
- Pro

Avoid names that are:

- too mystical
- too clinical
- too technical
- too game-like
- too sales-heavy

Bad names:

- Soul Engine
- Mood Optimizer
- Healing Score
- AI Therapist
- Inner Truth Unlock
- Mind XP

## Buttons

Buttons should tell the user exactly what happens.

Rules:

- Start with a verb when possible.
- Keep labels 1 to 4 words.
- Avoid emotional promises.
- Avoid "Continue" unless the next destination is obvious.
- Avoid "Submit" unless it is a form with no better action.

Approved button patterns:

- Write a note
- Save note
- Reflect with AI
- Refresh with AI
- Export notes
- See Pro options
- Join Pro waitlist
- Delete note
- Delete account
- Cancel
- Try again

Avoid:

- Unlock
- Transform
- Start journey
- Analyze me
- Optimize
- Level up
- Claim insight

## Links

Links should be descriptive.

Good:

- "Read the privacy policy"
- "See Pro options"
- "Return to notes"

Avoid:

- "Click here"
- "Learn more" when the destination is ambiguous

## Empty States

Empty states should do three things:

1. Name what is empty.
2. Normalize it.
3. Offer one clear next action if useful.

Template:

> [What is empty]. [Why that is okay or expected]. [Next action].

Examples:

- "No notes yet. Start with a few lines whenever you're ready."
- "No tags yet. Tags will appear as you add them to notes."
- "The Life Wiki is quiet for now. Write 3 entries before it can say anything useful."

Avoid:

- "No data."
- "Nothing found."
- "You haven't started your journey."
- "Unlock insights by journaling more."

## Loading States

Loading labels should be short and truthful.

Good:

- Saving...
- Reflecting...
- Refreshing...
- Loading notes...
- Preparing export...

For longer AI actions:

- "Refreshing your Life Wiki..."
- "Looking for patterns in your saved notes..."
- "This may take a moment."

Avoid:

- "Reading your soul..."
- "Mining emotional data..."
- "Generating psychological profile..."

## Success States

Use quiet confirmation.

Good:

- Saved.
- Deleted.
- Export ready.
- Reflection refreshed.
- Your note is safe.

Avoid:

- "Great job!"
- "You're doing amazing!"
- "Another step complete!"

## Error States

Every error should include:

1. What happened.
2. Whether user data is safe.
3. What the user can do next.

Examples:

- "I couldn't save this just now. Please try again."
- "The reflection could not load. Your note is unchanged."
- "Your session expired. Sign in again to continue."
- "The Life Wiki could not refresh. You can try again in a moment."

Avoid:

- "Error."
- "Bad request."
- "User failed validation."
- "Something went wrong" with no next step.

## AI Copy Rules

### User-Facing AI Labels

Use "AI" plainly when the feature uses AI.

Approved:

- Reflect with AI
- Refresh with AI
- AI reflection
- Optional AI support

Avoid:

- Magic
- Oracle
- Therapist
- Brain
- Personal psychologist
- Deep mind engine

### AI Reflection Output

AI reflections should:

- mirror the user's language lightly
- name possible patterns
- ask 1 useful question at most
- avoid diagnosis
- avoid certainty
- avoid generic advice
- avoid long conclusions
- stay grounded in the note

Good structure:

1. One observation.
2. One possible pattern.
3. One gentle question or return point.

Example:

> This entry seems to carry both tiredness and relief. You sound worn down by how much had to be held together, but there is also a small moment of steadiness in the way you named what mattered. If you return to this later, you might ask what gave you that steadiness.

Bad:

> You are clearly experiencing burnout and need to change your work-life balance immediately.

### AI Disclaimers

Use disclaimers where needed, but do not make every AI surface feel legalistic.

Good:

> AI reflections are for personal reflection. They are not medical advice.

Bad:

> The AI system provides advanced therapeutic guidance.

## Privacy Copy Rules

Privacy copy must be:

- concrete
- plain
- accurate
- humble

Good:

- "Your notes belong to you."
- "We do not use your notes to train AI models."
- "AI features run only when you choose them."
- "Deleting your notes removes them from your Reflections account."

Avoid:

- "Your privacy is our top priority" by itself.
- "Completely secure."
- "Military-grade" unless verified and relevant.
- "Anonymous" if auth or account data exists.

## Billing And Pro Copy Rules

Pro copy should explain access without emotional leverage.

Good:

> Pro keeps writing unlimited and lets you refresh AI-supported views whenever you ask.

Good:

> You can still read what is already here. Upgrade when you want ongoing AI refreshes.

Avoid:

> Upgrade to unlock your healing.

Avoid:

> Your deeper self-understanding is waiting.

## Quota Copy Rules

Limits should be clear and calm.

Good:

- "The free plan includes 30 notes each month."
- "You have used your free AI reflection."
- "You have used your free Life Wiki refresh."
- "You can still read what is already here."

Avoid:

- "Limit exceeded."
- "Upgrade required to continue your journey."
- "You ran out of insight."

## Notification And Reminder Rules

Reminders should be opt-in and gentle.

Allowed:

- "A quiet reminder to write, if you want it."
- "Your evening writing reminder is on."

Avoid:

- "You missed your reflection."
- "Don't break your streak."
- "You haven't journaled in 3 days."

## Accessibility Writing

Accessible copy is part of brand quality.

Rules:

- Icon-only buttons must have clear accessible labels.
- Error messages must be specific.
- Form labels must exist even if placeholders are present.
- Do not rely on color words alone.
- Avoid vague instructions like "fix the highlighted fields."

Good:

- "Email is required."
- "Password must be at least 8 characters."
- "Open menu"
- "Close dialog"
- "Delete note"

## Forms

Labels should be short and concrete.

Good:

- Email
- Password
- Note title
- Tags
- Mood

Avoid:

- Tell us your email address
- Your secret access phrase
- Emotional category

## Dates And Numbers

Rules:

- Use numerals for limits and counts: "3 entries", "30 notes".
- Use plain time periods: "this month", "each month".
- Avoid gamified count language: "score", "points", "level".
- Use exact dates in policy or billing contexts.
