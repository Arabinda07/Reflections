# Glossary

This glossary defines canonical product terms for Reflections. Use these terms consistently in UI, docs, tests, onboarding, marketing, and support copy.

## Product Name

### Reflections

The product name.

Rules:

- Always capitalize as `Reflections`.
- Do not write `reflections` when referring to the product.
- Do not add generic suffixes like `Reflections AI` unless a future naming decision creates a separate product.

Approved:

- "Welcome to Reflections."
- "Your notes in Reflections belong to you."

Avoid:

- "Reflections AI"
- "The Reflections platform" in user-facing UI
- "Mindful AI Journal" as a product name

## Core Objects

### Note

A single journal entry created by the user.

Use:

- note
- saved note
- journal note when extra clarity is needed

Avoid:

- content
- post
- data point
- record, unless in technical docs

### Reflection

A reflective output or act of thinking, depending on context.

Rules:

- Use carefully because the product name is Reflections.
- For AI output, prefer `AI reflection`.
- For user writing, prefer `note` unless the sentence is conceptual.

### Entry

A human-friendly synonym for note, especially when describing counts or minimums.

Use:

- "Write 3 entries..."
- "Your first entry..."

Avoid:

- using entry and note inconsistently in the same component if it creates confusion

### Mood

An optional user-selected emotional label.

Rules:

- Do not imply mood is a clinical measurement.
- Do not turn mood into a score.

Approved:

- "Mood"
- "Mood patterns"

Avoid:

- "Mental health score"
- "Emotional rating"

### Tag

A user-created or suggested label for grouping notes.

Rules:

- Keep lowercase unless the tag is a proper noun.
- Do not call tags categories unless building a separate category system.

## AI Terms

### AI

Use plainly for features that call an AI model.

Rules:

- Do not hide AI when it is active.
- Do not overuse AI as the product's identity.

Approved:

- Reflect with AI
- Refresh with AI
- Optional AI support

Avoid:

- AI-powered as the main product headline
- magic
- intelligence engine

### Reflect With AI

The explicit action for requesting an AI reflection on a note.

Rules:

- Use exactly `Reflect with AI` for the primary action.
- Do not rename to `Analyze`, `Diagnose`, or `Generate insight`.

### AI Reflection

The AI-generated reflection output.

Rules:

- Must be framed as supportive reflection, not advice or diagnosis.
- Must not imply professional mental health support.

### Refresh With AI

The explicit action for refreshing AI-supported Life Wiki or insight surfaces.

Rules:

- Use when the user asks the system to regenerate an AI-supported view.
- Avoid "Sync", "Process", or "Mine".

### Optional AI Support

The product-level framing for AI.

Rules:

- Use in landing, FAQ, privacy, and onboarding.
- This phrase helps prevent AI-first drift.

Avoid:

- "AI companion that watches your journey"
- "automatic AI support" unless a specific opt-in mode exists and is described clearly

## Insight Terms

### Insights

The dashboard area for rhythm, mood, tags, tasks, and Life Wiki access.

Rules:

- Insights are observations, not scores.
- Do not call this Analytics in user-facing copy.

### Pattern

A recurring theme, behavior, emotional loop, or topic that appears across notes.

Rules:

- Use tentatively unless well-supported.
- Do not overclaim from 1 note.

Approved:

- "A few patterns may be emerging."
- "Patterns become more useful as you write."

Avoid:

- "We know your pattern."
- "This proves a pattern."

### Signal

The amount of meaningful writing available for useful reflection.

Rules:

- Use sparingly.
- Good in low-data states.

Approved:

- "The Life Wiki needs more signal before it can say anything useful."

Avoid:

- "Data signal"
- "User signal quality"

## Life Wiki / Sanctuary Terms

### Life Wiki

The user-facing name for the structured personal wiki built from reflections.

Rules:

- Use `Life Wiki` in product UI where clarity matters.
- It should feel understandable, not mystical.
- It is a personal meaning library, not raw journal storage.

### Sanctuary

The broader product metaphor and deeper system concept.

Rules:

- Use sparingly in user-facing UI.
- Good in docs and architectural language.
- Do not overuse as a generic button label.

Approved:

- "writing sanctuary"
- "Reflections Sanctuary" in docs

Avoid:

- "Enter Sanctuary" as a primary CTA
- "Sanctuary intelligence"

### The Mirror

Internal/product narrative term for free-tier non-AI or low-AI pattern surfaces.

Rules:

- Mostly internal.
- If user-facing, explain plainly.

### The Librarian

Internal/product narrative term for AI-maintained wiki behavior.

Rules:

- Use in technical or conceptual docs.
- Avoid in core UI unless the feature has been intentionally branded.

### Smart Mode

Potential account-level opt-in for ongoing AI synthesis.

Rules:

- Use only if the implemented product has this explicit toggle.
- Must explain what turns on, what stays private, and how to turn it off.
- Do not use if AI is only on-demand in the current surface.

## Plan And Billing Terms

### Free Plan

The no-paid-access plan.

Rules:

- Do not make it sound lesser as a human experience.
- Explain limits plainly.

Approved:

- "The free plan includes 30 notes each month."

Avoid:

- "Basic"
- "Limited self-discovery"

### Pro

The paid plan.

Rules:

- Use `Pro`, not `Premium`, unless code or existing plan naming requires it.
- Pro expands room and continuity.
- Pro does not sell healing.

Approved:

- "Pro keeps writing unlimited."
- "See Pro options."

Avoid:

- "Unlock healing"
- "Premium mental clarity"

### Monthly Wellness Journey

The calmer framing for monthly limits and continuity.

Rules:

- Use when explaining monthly usage in a human way.
- Do not use as a gamified progress mechanic.

### Upgrade

The act of moving from Free to Pro.

Rules:

- Acceptable in billing UI.
- Pair with calm explanatory copy.

Avoid:

- "Unlock your journey"
- "Upgrade or stop"

## Privacy Terms

### Private

Use when describing the user's notes and account space.

Rules:

- Must be backed by actual access behavior.
- Avoid vague overuse.

### Your Notes Belong To You

Approved core privacy phrase.

Use in:

- privacy page
- onboarding
- account/export surfaces

### Training Data

Use when explaining AI model training.

Approved:

- "We do not use your notes to train AI models."

Avoid:

- "Your data is never used" if analytics or storage exists.

## Naming Tests

Before approving a new name, ask:

1. Would a tired user understand it?
2. Does it overpromise?
3. Does it sound clinical?
4. Does it sound gamified?
5. Does it make AI the hero?
6. Could it appear in a privacy policy without sounding evasive?
7. Does it still feel calm on a small mobile screen?
