# Tone

Tone is how the voice adapts to context. Reflections should remain recognizable across every surface, but a save toast, an error, an AI reflection, and an upgrade prompt should not sound identical.

## Tone Principles

1. Match the user's emotional state.
2. Say only what is useful.
3. Do not over-celebrate private reflection.
4. Do not dramatize distress.
5. Do not hide system limits.
6. Do not make AI sound more certain than it is.
7. Keep sensitive moments slower, plainer, and less decorative.

## Tone Map By User Moment

| User Moment | Tone | Product Role | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| First visit | calm, clear | explain the promise | hype |
| Sign-up | reassuring | create trust | long privacy lectures |
| Empty journal | inviting | lower starting friction | guilt |
| Writing | quiet | stay out of the way | suggestions everywhere |
| Saving | brief | confirm safety | celebration |
| AI reflection | warm, tentative | mirror patterns | diagnosis |
| Insights | observant | show signals | scoring |
| Low data | patient | normalize early state | "insufficient" |
| Error | steady | explain next step | blame |
| Paywall | factual, gentle | explain access | pressure |
| Account deletion | serious | confirm consequences | softness that obscures risk |
| Privacy | plain | build trust | vague reassurance |

## Like This / Not This

### Sensitive User Story

Like this:

> Reflections can help you return to difficult thoughts at your own pace. Your writing stays private, and optional AI support only appears when you ask for it.

Not this:

> Reflections uses AI to uncover your deepest emotional blocks and guide your healing journey.

Why: The first respects pace and consent. The second overclaims and sounds clinical.

### Low-Entry Insights

Like this:

> Write 2 more entries before the Life Wiki can say anything useful.

Not this:

> Not enough data. Add more journal entries to unlock insights.

Why: The first is concrete and calm. The second sounds transactional and data-centric.

### AI Failure

Like this:

> I couldn't refresh the Life Wiki just now. Your notes are unchanged.

Not this:

> AI processing failed.

Why: The first reassures the user and explains impact. The second is cold and unclear.

### Upgrade Prompt

Like this:

> You can still read what is already here. Pro lets you keep refreshing the Life Wiki with AI whenever you ask.

Not this:

> Upgrade now to unlock deeper self-discovery.

Why: The first is honest and non-punitive. The second uses emotional leverage.

### Save Confirmation

Like this:

> Saved.

Not this:

> Incredible reflection saved! You're growing every day!

Why: Private writing does not need applause.

## Tone By Surface

### Landing Page

Tone: clear, elegant, restrained.

Use:

- private
- quiet
- write
- notice
- return
- optional
- patterns
- room

Avoid:

- revolutionary
- unlock
- transform
- AI-powered as the headline idea
- mental health optimization
- best self

Example:

> A quiet place to write and notice what keeps returning.

### FAQ

Tone: helpful, plain, specific.

Use direct answers. Avoid mystique.

Good:

> Does AI run automatically?
> No. AI-supported reflections and Life Wiki refreshes run only when you choose them.

Bad:

> Our AI gently works in the background to understand you.

### Create Note

Tone: minimal, focused, almost invisible.

Good:

- "Save note"
- "Reflect with AI"
- "A few lines are enough."
- "Saved."

Bad:

- "Analyze entry"
- "Generate therapeutic insight"
- "Complete today's reflection"

### AI Reflection Modal

Tone: warm, reflective, bounded.

Good:

> This seems to be less about the event itself and more about how much energy it took to stay composed.

Bad:

> Your anxiety response shows a deep need for validation.

### Insights

Tone: observational, not evaluative.

Good:

> Your writing has leaned toward work and rest this month.

Bad:

> Your mental health score improved.

### Account

Tone: factual, respectful.

Good:

> The free plan includes 30 notes each month, one AI reflection, and one Life Wiki refresh after there is enough writing to support it.

Bad:

> Upgrade to take full control of your wellness.

### Privacy Policy

Tone: plain, responsible.

Good:

> We do not use your personal notes to train AI models.

Bad:

> Your data is sacred to us.

## Emotional Intensity Rules

### When The User Is Beginning

Use gentle invitation.

Examples:

- "Begin with what you remember."
- "A few lines are enough."
- "You do not need to make this complete."

### When The User Has Written Something

Use quiet confirmation.

Examples:

- "Saved."
- "Your note is here."
- "You can return to this later."

### When The User Asks For AI

Use a reflective but tentative tone.

Examples:

- "One pattern that may be present..."
- "This seems connected to..."
- "You might return to..."

### When The User Has Little Data

Use patience.

Examples:

- "This view needs a little more writing before it can be useful."
- "Patterns are easier to see after a few entries."

### When Something Fails

Use steadiness.

Examples:

- "This did not save. Please try again."
- "The reflection could not load. Your note is unchanged."
- "Your connection may have dropped."

### When The User Is Deleting Data

Use clarity over softness.

Examples:

- "This permanently deletes your saved notes from Reflections."
- "This cannot be undone."
- "Your sign-in account may still need to be closed by support."

## Punctuation

- Use periods for most confirmations.
- Use question marks for prompts that genuinely ask the user to choose.
- Use exclamation points rarely.
- Do not use multiple exclamation points.
- Avoid ellipses except in loading labels.
- Avoid emoji in core product copy unless a future brand decision explicitly allows them.

## Capitalization

- Use sentence case for buttons, headings, labels, and helper text.
- Use title case only for proper names or formal document headings.
- Do not use all caps for emotional emphasis.
- Do not shout errors.

## Humor

Humor is not a primary voice tool for Reflections.

Acceptable:

- light, human phrasing in non-sensitive utility moments

Avoid:

- jokes in errors
- jokes in privacy, billing, deletion, or emotional content
- mascot-like personality
- sarcasm

## Urgency

Use urgency only when:

- payment state requires immediate clarity
- deletion is irreversible
- session/security requires action
- data may be lost

Do not use urgency for:

- engagement
- upgrade prompts
- writing reminders
- AI feature discovery
