# Reflections Investment Memo

## One sentence

Reflections is building Private AI Memory, starting with private writing as the wedge.

## Two-sentence explanation

Personal AI needs durable personal context, but the most valuable context is often too private for generic AI tools. Reflections earns that context through calm private writing, then turns it into useful memory through Invited AI.

## Category definition

Working category: Private AI Memory.

Plain definition: software that helps users turn private, user-owned life context into useful memory through Invited AI.

Category alternatives to revisit later: personal AI context layer, user-owned memory graph, personal memory infrastructure.

## World shift

AI is moving from answers to context. The scarce asset is not another model interface; it is trusted, user-owned personal context.

## Core problem

Private life context is valuable but poorly preserved. Notes, moods, decisions, relationship details, and recurring patterns are scattered, forgotten, or placed in tools users may not trust with intimate data.

## Core insight

Trust is not the destination. Trust enables more honest writing, honest writing creates richer context, richer context creates better memory, and better memory should drive retention and paid demand.

## Wedge

Private writing is the entry point because people already use writing to capture emotional, relational, and decision context. Reflections should feel private before it feels automated.

## Expansion

Writing expands into Life Wiki, RelationshipOS, decision trails, reminders, AI reflection, and eventually a Personal Memory OS.

Concrete memory examples:

- Six months later, I can see why I stopped trusting someone.
- A decision today can be compared with a similar decision from last year.
- Relationship notes become a pattern, not scattered entries.
- Mood and writing history explain what changed before a major life decision.

## Why now

AI is becoming more useful when it has personal context, while users are becoming more cautious about where that context lives. That tension creates room for a private, user-owned memory layer.

## Why us

Reflections already has the product wedge: private writing, Life Wiki, AI reflection, relationship context, and a restrained product philosophy. The company understands that AI should be invited, not ambient.

## What this round proves

This round should prove that private writing can create memory-driven retention and paid demand.

The proof path is 10-15 user interviews, 20-50 beta users, onboarding source tracking, Week 1 retention, notes per active user, Life Wiki and AI reflection usage, and willingness-to-pay signal.

## Biggest risks

- The category stays abstract.
- Users value privacy but do not pay for it.
- Memory features do not improve retention.
- Generic AI tools copy the surface area.
- RelationshipOS feels like a second product instead of expansion.

## Biggest assumptions

- Private writing is the best trust wedge for personal AI memory.
- Users will add more honest context when AI is invited and bounded.
- Longitudinal context creates a better product over time.
- The memory graph becomes harder to copy than individual features.

## Success metrics

- Week 1 and Week 4 retention.
- Notes written per active user.
- Life Wiki and AI reflection usage.
- RelationshipOS repeat usage.
- Free-to-Pro conversion.
- User interviews linking trust, memory, and willingness to pay.
