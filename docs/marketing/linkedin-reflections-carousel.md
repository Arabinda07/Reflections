# LinkedIn Carousel: Reflections

## Goal

Create a high-quality LinkedIn carousel that introduces Reflections as a private, writing-first AI journal.

The post should make one point clearly: Reflections keeps the note at the center. AI, mood labels, prompts, tags, Life Wiki, and Insights support the writing instead of taking over the experience.

## Voice Rules

- Sound human, grounded, and specific.
- Keep slides sparse. One headline, one supporting line, one visual idea.
- Do not overuse soft-brand words such as "quiet", "calm", or "gentle".
- Avoid generic wellness claims, therapy framing, productivity framing, and AI hype.
- Avoid "unlock", "transform", "optimize", "crush", "journey", "companion", "therapist", and "self-improvement".

## Carousel Specs

- Platform: LinkedIn
- Format: 7-slide carousel
- Size: 1080 x 1080
- Visual system: warm paper base, botanical green accent, refined editorial spacing
- Type: Manrope for headlines and labels, Spectral italic for rare emotional emphasis

## Slide Direction

### Slide 1

Headline:
**A private place to write**

Supporting line:
AI only when you ask.

Visual:
Full-bleed warm paper field with a cropped product/editor surface. Use botanical green only as a small identity mark or focus ring. Keep the slide almost still.

### Slide 2

Headline:
**Most journal apps push.**

Supporting line:
Streaks. Scores. Prompts that do not know when to stop.

Visual:
Use faint, crossed-out UI fragments: a streak badge, a score pill, a notification dot. Keep them low contrast so the slide does not become a rant.

### Slide 3

Headline:
**Reflections starts with the note.**

Supporting line:
Everything else supports the writing.

Visual:
Show the editor or a note surface as the primary subject. Secondary features should sit at the edge, smaller and visually subordinate.

### Slide 4

Headline:
**Not a chatbot with a journal attached.**

Supporting line:
The entry stays at the center.

Visual:
Avoid chat bubbles. Use a centered note plane with AI controls shown as an explicit action, not a persistent character or assistant panel.

### Slide 5

Headline:
**AI is invited, not ambient.**

Supporting line:
You choose when it reflects.

Visual:
Feature a clear "Reflect with AI" style action. Make the button visible but not oversized. The point is control, not spectacle.

### Slide 6

Headline:
**Patterns take time.**

Supporting line:
No diagnosis. No performance score.

Visual:
Use a restrained Life Wiki or Insights motif: small linked notes, tags, or month markers. Avoid medical charts, mood scores, and dramatic before/after visuals.

### Slide 7

Headline:
**Reflections**

Supporting line:
For thoughts that need somewhere to land.

Visual:
Brand close. Use a clean product screenshot, app icon, or editorial cover frame. Add only one small CTA: "Follow the build" or "See the app".

## LinkedIn Caption

```text
I am building Reflections as a different kind of AI journal.

Not a chatbot with a notebook attached.
Not a streak app.
Not another place that turns private writing into performance.

The note stays at the center.

Mood labels, tags, prompts, Life Wiki, Insights, and AI reflections are there to support the writing, not replace it.

AI only enters when the user asks. It should help notice patterns with care, with limits, and without pretending to understand more than it does.

That design choice changes the whole app:
less pressure,
clearer privacy language,
fewer dopamine loops,
more room for the user's own meaning.

Reflections is for the moment when a thought needs somewhere to land.

I am sharing the direction in this carousel.
```

## Production Notes

- Keep each slide under 14 visible words when possible.
- Use one dominant visual per slide.
- Let product surfaces carry the story. Do not fill the canvas with explanatory text.
- Use botanical green for primary actions, identity marks, or focus states only.
- Keep body copy away from the edges. Use generous margins and stable alignment.
- If screenshots are used, crop to one clear product moment rather than showing a whole dense screen.

## Quality Checklist

- No slide depends on a paragraph to make sense.
- No therapy, diagnosis, or clinical authority is implied.
- No vague privacy reassurance is used.
- No "AI-powered" headline.
- No repeated reliance on "quiet", "calm", or "gentle".
- No stock wellness imagery, motivational quote styling, chat-bubble metaphor, or cluttered feature grid.
- Caption reads like a founder explaining a design decision, not a SaaS launch template.
