# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Reflections is for people who want a private, calm place to write, name what they are feeling, and return to their own thoughts without being scored or rushed. They may arrive tired, scattered, reflective, anxious, hopeful, or simply curious. Many will use it in quiet personal moments: before bed, after work, during a pause in the day, or when a thought needs somewhere safe to land.

## Product Purpose

Reflections is a writing-first wellness journal. Its core promise is a quiet place to write privately and, when the user asks, a gentle way to notice patterns inside that writing. The journal entry is the primary object. Mood labels, tags, tasks, prompts, Life Wiki, Insights, and AI reflections exist to support writing, not to replace it or become the center of gravity.

Success means the product helps users begin where they are, keep ownership of their notes, understand product limits clearly, and return to their writing without pressure mechanics, social comparison, or hidden automation.

## Brand Personality

Reflections should feel calm, human, grounded, spacious, and trustworthy. It should sound like a thoughtful product made by someone who respects emotional nuance, not like a wellness brand performing intimacy or an AI assistant trying to sound profound.

The brand is refined and quiet, but not empty. It should feel like a well-made private notebook combined with a careful editorial interface: warm enough to invite writing, plain enough to earn trust, and restrained enough to let the user's own meaning remain central.

## Anti-references

Reflections must not become:

- a habit game, streak app, leaderboard, XP loop, or productivity tracker
- a therapy replacement, diagnostic tool, crisis service, or clinical authority
- an AI chatbot with a journal attached
- a motivational coach using shame, urgency, or exaggerated self-improvement language
- a social network, public feed, or virality surface
- a generic wellness app full of inspirational slogans
- a product that treats private writing as data inventory
- a dark-pattern subscription surface that implies care or self-understanding requires payment

Avoid language such as "unlock your best self", "transform your mental health", "AI-powered" as the main promise, "analyze my soul", "optimize mood", "insufficient data", and vague privacy reassurance without specifics.

## Design Principles

1. Writing is the sanctuary. Every surface should protect the user's ability to write without distraction.
2. AI is invited, not ambient. Use explicit actions, bounded claims, and tentative interpretation.
3. Calm is behavior. The product should lower pressure through pacing, copy, motion, and hierarchy.
4. Privacy must be concrete. Say what is stored, what leaves the app, what can be deleted, and what cannot be promised.
5. Patterns emerge slowly. Insights should be observant and patient, never evaluative or score-like.
6. Premium is additive. Pro should expand continuity and convenience without making free users feel punished.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Reflections should meet WCAG AA for functional text, controls, and public pages. Focus states must be visible. Dialogs must manage focus appropriately. Icon-only controls require accessible names. Reduced-motion preferences must be respected. Legal, privacy, billing, deletion, and AI copy should be especially plain, specific, and readable.
